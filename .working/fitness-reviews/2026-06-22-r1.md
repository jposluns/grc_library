## 3. Page-by-Page Findings

Findings grouped by file. Each finding shows persona(s), severity, location, evidence quote, impact, and recommendation. **All findings are `verification: unverified` pending Pass-1 verification.**

### `README.md`

**FR-112** — Maintainer-context leakage in adopter-narrative (P1, Medium, line 58). The README's "What this repository is" section emphasises Claude Code and the audit toolchain as if they're required dependencies; clarification needed that they're optional by-products. *Recommendation:* reframe to lead with "the corpus can be adopted standalone" and de-emphasise toolchain in the opening narrative.

**FR-113** — Repository Structure acronyms unexpanded (P10, Medium, lines 108, 123). The Repository Structure block uses "CAPA" and "SIEM" without first-occurrence expansion, regressing the acronym-clarity pattern PRs #172 and #179 established. *Recommendation:* expand "CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)" and "SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)" at first occurrence.

### `governance/register-digital-trust-and-assurance-metrics.md`

**FR-114** — DTI tier vocabulary inconsistent with framework CMMI (P1, High[critical], line 65). DTI uses 4-tier non-CMMI naming (Developing/Managed/Integrated/Optimized); the governance-performance framework uses 5-tier CMMI (Initial/Managed/Defined/Quantitatively Managed/Optimized). Closely tied to FR-14 from r1 which is still pending. *Recommendation:* align DTI thresholds to the 5-tier CMMI ladder; same fix as FR-14.

### `governance/register-role-authority.md`

**FR-115** — Risk Owner role missing from Role Authority Register (P3, High, lines 27-62). PR #178 added Risk Owner to ERM standard §3 but did NOT add a row to the canonical Role Authority Register. The standard's role definition has no cross-reference back to the register; the register cannot serve as a single source of truth for the role's RACI. *Recommendation:* add Risk Owner row to the Role Authority Register with accountability description, evidence expectations, and reciprocal cross-reference to ERM standard §3.

### `risk/standard-enterprise-risk-management.md`

**FR-116** — Risk Owner monitoring cadence incomplete for non-Critical risks (P6, Medium, line 192). §8.1 specifies monthly cadence for Critical risks only; High/Moderate/Low cadences are implicit (the template says "quarterly status update for all open risks" but the standard does not affirm it). *Recommendation:* extend §8.1 to specify Risk Owner reporting cadence by score band: Critical monthly, High monthly, Moderate quarterly, Low annual.

**FR-117** — Risk Owner evidence expectations implicit (P3 + P4, Medium, line 203-216). §9 lists evidence outputs but does not specify what evidence proves Risk Owner accountability execution (confirmation, treatment selection, plan ownership, monitoring, status reporting). *Recommendation:* expand §9 with explicit evidence requirements for each Risk Owner accountability action.

### `risk/procedure-risk-register.md` (cross-doc with standard)

**FR-118** — FR-12 cross-document treatment vocabulary divergence still open (P3 + P6, High, lines 34, 55). Standard's §6 (PR #178) defines Avoid/Mitigate/Transfer/Accept/Exploit/Enhance; procedure's "Select Treatment" step defines Mitigate/Avoid/Transfer/Accept/Monitor/Further-Analysis. Six-option sets don't overlap. *Recommendation:* designate the standard as canonical; align the procedure to the standard's set; document where "Monitor"/"Further Analysis" map.

### `governance/policy-exception-and-risk-acceptance-management.md`

**FR-119** — "Risk Owner" used with different responsibility set than ERM standard (P7, Medium, line 46). The exception policy assigns Risk Owner: "Validates the risk assessment, confirms residual exposure, and ensures alignment with enterprise risk appetite" — different from the ERM standard's definition of the same-named role. *Recommendation:* either explicitly state the two are the same role (and add the exception-validation responsibility to ERM standard scope), or rename one to disambiguate (e.g., "Exception Risk Validator").

**FR-120** — 180-day baseline citation imprecise/circular (P3 + P7, Medium, line 99). §3.7.1 (PR #179) anchors the 180-day baseline to "NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 CA-6 ongoing authorisation maintenance" and "ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.2 internal-audit cycles". Neither source explicitly mandates 180-day intervals; CA-6 specifies annual+, ISO 27001:2022 Clause 9.2 does not prescribe interval. Reasoning is circular ("180 days because semi-annual"). *Recommendation:* either cite specific normative language that supports the 180-day choice, or reframe as "library convention chosen for operational alignment" without claiming external mandate.

### `security/procedure-access-control.md`

**FR-121** — Emergency-access "material harm" undefined (P2 + P6 + P7, High, line 64). §1.4(b) cites "material business or safety harm" as a trigger condition but never defines "material". Three personas surfaced this independently. An approver has no objective basis to evaluate the trigger; the CISO's quarterly review (§1.4.3) has no standard to apply retroactively. *Recommendation:* either define "material" with thresholds (e.g., affects Tier-1 service; loss of service availability) or reference the incident-response P1/P2 severity matrix.

**FR-122** — "Declared incident response" not tied to specific status (P6 + P7, High, line 64). §1.4(a) cites "a declared incident response is active" but the incident-response procedure defines P1/P2/P3/P4 classification without establishing a "declaration" event or status field. *Recommendation:* tie the trigger to a specific incident status, e.g., "an incident has been assigned a P1 or P2 severity classification and an Incident Commander has been appointed".

**FR-123** — "Delegated security lead" undefined in roles table (P6, High, lines 35-39). §1.3 and §1.4 cite "delegated security lead" as an approver but the Roles and Responsibilities table does not include the role. *Recommendation:* either add Delegated Security Lead to the roles table with explicit scope, or remove the phrase and require CISO-only approval.

**FR-124** — Access-review revocation timeline contradiction (P2, Medium, lines 3.2-3.3). §3.2 says unjustified access is "revoked under the next access review" (annual for standard user — up to 12 months); §3.3 says "access that cannot be justified is revoked immediately". The mutual exclusivity is unclear. *Recommendation:* clarify §3.2 to require immediate revocation, OR document the deferred-revocation justification.

**FR-125** — Emergency-access revocation enforcement lacks escalation (P2, High, §1.4.2). The 24-hour revocation is operationally brittle: if Identity Team misses the deadline, procedure is silent on escalation. *Recommendation:* add an escalation clause mirroring §1.2.1-1.2.4 — automated alert at 23-hour mark; non-revocation past 24 hours triggers a security incident investigation.

**FR-126** — Auto-escalation mechanic vague (P7, Medium, lines 54-58). The passive voice "is auto-escalated" doesn't say who or what triggers escalation (ITSM system automation? manual review?). *Recommendation:* explicitly state automation expectation, or document the manual backstop control with assigned ownership.

### `security/framework-zero-trust-architecture.md`

**FR-127** — TLS 1.2 specified vs TLS 1.3 in encryption policy (P2, High, line 75). ZTA framework's Pillar 3 specifies "TLS 1.2 or above"; the encryption policy mandates "TLS 1.3 or stronger". TLS 1.2 has reached end-of-life guidance from NIST and OWASP 2025. *Recommendation:* align ZTA framework to TLS 1.3+ to match encryption policy.

### `governance/register-data-retention-schedule.md`

**FR-128** — Retention chain break: CAPA records 5y vs control-testing 7y (P4, High, line 66). PR #179's FR-25 raised control-testing evidence to 7y but adjacent CAPA records (which document remediation of audit findings) still at 5y. Evidence chain breaks at year 5 when CAPA expires but control test evidence remains. *Recommendation:* raise CAPA retention to 7y.

**FR-129** — Retention chain break: internal audit reports 5y vs procedures' 7y (P4, High, line 83). Data-retention register shows internal audit reports at 5y, but both `compliance/standard-internal-audit.md:360` and `compliance/procedure-audit-planning.md:447` mandate 7y for audit working papers, evidence, draft findings, management responses, and final reports. *Recommendation:* raise internal audit reports retention in register to 7y; verify "External audit and certification records" (currently "Certification period + 5 years") is intentionally shorter.

### `docs/decision-tree.md`

**FR-130** — Entry sequence delays portal encounter (P8, Medium, lines 107-120). Section 2.1 "Orientation (read first)" puts portal at item 8 of 8; an adopter reads 20-30 minutes of orientation material before encountering the portal that the README and the portal itself declare canonical. *Recommendation:* move portal reference to item 1 of section 2.1; recommend README and adopter-guide as optional deeper dives.

### `docs/template-quickstart.md` and `docs/adopter-guide.md`

**FR-131** — Quickstart vs adopter-guide procedure-set divergence (P8, Low, line 39 vs lines 116-117). Quickstart names `risk/procedure-risk-register.md` as the sole 6-artefact floor; adopter-guide's Tier 1 names `risk/procedure-risk-assessment-methodology.md` AND `risk/procedure-risk-acceptance.md` (not the register procedure). *Recommendation:* reconcile to a single Tier 1 risk-artefact set across entry points.
