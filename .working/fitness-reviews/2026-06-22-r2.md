# Library Fitness Review: 2026-06-22 r2 (trust-recovery mode)

## Executive Summary

Run r2 is the `/fitness` half of a maintainer-directed trust-recovery suite (paired with `/full-qa` deep-qa-review iter 1, run first). Ten persona subagents reviewed the whole corpus fresh; mechanical baseline was green (`run_all_audits.sh` exit 0, 46/46, on a full clone after the takeover container's shallow clone was corrected). In trust-recovery mode every confirmed finding routes to TODO P1 top tagged `[fitness:persona]`, bypassing the normal Pass-2 bucket-deferral; the maintainer triages at sign-off.

Overall: the corpus remains broadly fit, with strong areas confirmed by multiple personas (PQC posture, AI/agentic security depth, DSAR/breach workflow coverage, threat-modelling, metadata-block uniformity, naming-prefix taxonomy, jurisdiction-index integrity, generator sync). The findings concentrate in **six convergent multi-surface contradictions** that mechanical gates do not catch, four of them H[critical]:

1. **Risk scoring divergence** (P3+P7): the ERM standard and the assessment methodology define different score bands, rating labels, and likelihood scales; the same residual score yields a different rating, cadence, and escalation depending on which document an assessor opens.
2. **TLS floor contradiction** (P2+P7): the canonical encryption policy and the developer-requirements standard mandate TLS 1.3 and prohibit 1.2, while the security quick-reference ("check first") and the baseline-and-standards reference permit "TLS 1.2 (minimum)"; residual 1.2 surfaces also persist in mobile, the crypto pack rule, MCP-security, and production-security.
3. **Retention contradictions** (P4): the logging standard's 7-year floor for security/audit logs conflicts with the retention schedule (1-3y) and the records standard (IT/Security 1-3y); DSAR-record retention is 3y (schedule) vs 2y post-closure (records standard + DSR procedure).
4. **CPPA-not-in-force** (P9): DSR-rights tables, the breach matrix, and the privacy policy cite Canada's CPPA (and its section numbers) as operative law, while the Canada annex correctly states Bill C-27 lapsed and CPPA is not in force.
5. **DR RPO vs backup-gap self-contradiction** (P7): the DR plan sets a 1-hour Tier-1 RPO, permits a 24-hour backup gap, and declares any gap exceeding the RPO a P2 escalation, so a team meeting the backup rule is permanently in self-declared breach.
6. **Adopter starter-set divergence** (P1+P8): four entry points name four different "starter sets" (6 / 15 / 23 / ~37), two of them sharing the label "six-artefact core baseline" but listing different artefacts, and the 15-doc Tier 1 omits IAM and acceptable-use that the 6-doc floor calls non-negotiable.

Publication-readiness: the H[critical] contradictions (1-5) should be resolved before any external reliance event; each is a normative inconsistency that could drive mis-implementation or an audit non-compliance finding.

## Severity Model

Medium = confusion, inefficiency, inconsistent interpretation. Low = editorial. FYI = informational. The `[critical]` flag is a tag within the High tier.
