The findings concentrate in one mis-citation (a CCM control code that does not exist in v4.1) and a set of **cross-document value contradictions** that mechanical gates do not catch: the same fact (an acceptance authority, a retention period, an incident-notification clock, a regime in-force date, a likelihood-assignment basis) is stated differently in two or more documents, so an implementer's answer depends on which document they open. Eight findings are High and route to P1:

1. **CCM GRC-12 wrong citation** (FR-168): the policy-exception document cites "CCM v4.1 GRC-12 Exception Management" eight times, but GRC-12 is not a CCM v4.1 control (the GRC family stops at GRC-08; GRC-12 is an AICM "Ethics Committee" control). The correct control for a policy-exception process is GRC-04 "Policy Exception Process".
2. **ERM likelihood-description divergence** (FR-169): the ERM standard claims its likelihood scale matches the methodology, but the standard uses time-frequency anchors while the methodology uses threat-precedent anchors; labels and score thresholds match, the assignment basis does not.
3. **Risk 5x5-matrix pointer** (FR-170): the risk register template points to the methodology for the 5x5 rating matrix, which the methodology does not contain (only a threshold table); the visual matrix lives in the ERM standard and the template.
4. **High-residual acceptance-authority conflict** (FR-171): three documents name three different authorities for accepting a High residual risk (CRO vs Executive Management vs Executive Committee or Board Risk Committee).
5. **ROPA retention conflict** (FR-172): the record-of-processing-activities template states "at least three years"; the authoritative retention schedule states "Active + 5 years". Stricter-is-safer points to 5 years.
6. **Offboarding broken section cross-references** (FR-173): the onboarding-and-offboarding procedure cites the wrong section numbers for the privileged-access timeline and the deprovisioning checklist.
7. **Incident P2 CISO-timing conflict** (FR-174): three values for CISO notification on a P2 incident (4 hours vs 1 hour vs 2 hours), including an intra-document conflict. Stricter is 1 hour.
8. **India DPDPA in-force conflict** (FR-175): the jurisdiction index says the DPDPA "entered into force April 2025"; the India annex records notification 13 Nov 2025 with phased commencement into 2026 to 2027.

Publication-readiness: the High contradictions (FR-168 to FR-175) should be resolved before any external reliance event; each is a normative inconsistency that could drive mis-implementation or an audit non-compliance finding. The corpus passed all 54 mechanical gates, so this whole finding set is the gate-blind class the trust-recovery suite exists to surface, and it paired with the `/full-qa` iter-2 forensic pass, which was clean except the two minor matrix/register items FR-198 and FR-199.
