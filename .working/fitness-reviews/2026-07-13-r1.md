# Library fitness review, 2026-07-13 r1

Deep-assessment r3 Phase-3 (and Phase-6 adoptability) instrument. Proactive whole-project
use of `/fitness`, NOT a trust-recovery trigger; it inherits trust-recovery's routing (every
confirmed finding surfaced and tiered, none dropped), apply-time verification (each cited
location re-read at source before routing), and no-self-sign-off convention (findings feed the
r3 set and HOLD for maintainer sign-off at r3 Phase 8). Ten personas dispatched, whole corpus,
maintainer mental model stripped from every brief. Mechanical baseline: 69/69 green at the r3
base (Phase 2 of the r3 record). Full (non-shallow) clone confirmed.

## Executive Summary

The corpus remains broadly fit for use and is unusually well reconciled on the value classes
prior fitness passes hardened (retention periods, risk-scoring bands, breach-notification
clocks, key-rotation cadences, glossary registers). The genuine findings this run cluster in
one class the mechanical gates cannot see: **the same obligation carrying a different value or
scope across two or more normative documents**, where an operational or dedicated document
restates a number that drifts from the authoritative standard or policy. Two clear the High
bar (internal-audit independence with no board line; vulnerability-remediation SLA divergence
across three documents), and about nine clear Medium. None is a mechanical-gate defect; all
pass the existence, citation, currency, and parity gates and read as plausible.

One dedup result is load-bearing: the TLS-1.2-vs-1.3 finding (P2 + P5) is a **fresh carrier of
the already-closed FR-127 class**. FR-127 (closed in PR #193) reconciled the ZTA-framework /
quick-reference / baseline carriers; the `operations/procedure-media-handling-and-transport.md`
carrier has existed since the initial public release (blame: 03ca390, 2026-05-31) and was never
swept, so the FR-127 corpus-wide TLS reconciliation was incomplete. It routes fresh
(FR-202), cross-referenced to closed FR-127, and is the lesson worth carrying: a value-contradiction
fix must grep every carrier at bare-token width, not only the carriers the finding named.

## Review Method

Ten persona subagents (P1 first-time executive, P2 security practitioner, P3 GRC practitioner,
P4 auditor, P5 policy/standards editor, P6 process owner, P7 skeptical reader, P8 adoption
practitioner, P9 privacy/DPO, P10 newcomer/onboarding), each briefed with scope, focus
questions, exclusions, and the fresh-reader mental-model strip; each required to return
structured blocks with a `path:line` quote per finding. The orchestrator then Pass-1 verified
every routed finding by re-reading each cited location in the live corpus (verdict tags below),
deduped against the fitness open-backlog and TODO, and tiered for routing. Findings without a
re-read-confirmed `path:line` are not routed.

## Page-by-Page Findings

Grouped by severity; each carries its Pass-1 verdict.

### High

- **FR-200 (P3) internal-audit independence has no board / audit-committee line.**
  `compliance/standard-internal-audit.md` pins structural independence solely to the ERC, an
  executive forum: `:95` "must have a reporting line that permits direct escalation to the ERC";
  `:77` reports to the ERC quarterly; `:278` distributes the final report to "the auditee, the
  CAE/GRC Manager, the CISO ..., and the ERC". A whole-file grep finds no "board" or "audit
  committee". Yet `risk/register-assurance-map.md:23` ("The board, the audit committee, and the
  risk committee use the map ..."), `:148` ("Material gaps are reported to the board risk
  committee"), and `:180` presuppose the board/audit-committee interface, and
  `governance/register-role-authority.md:41` assigns the CAE audit-committee reporting. Both a
  third-line-independence doctrine gap and a cross-document contradiction.
  Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-201 (P7) vulnerability-remediation SLA divergence across three normative documents.**
  For identical CVSS bands: `dev-security/standard-software-composition-analysis.md:76-78`
  High/Med/Low = 30 / 90 / 180 days (metrics row `:231` MTTR High <= 30d);
  `security/procedure-vulnerability-management.md:91-93` = 14 / 30 / 90 days, with scope `:23`,`:29`
  explicitly including "third-party components";
  `dev-security/standard-developer-security-requirements.md:175` sets SCA High = 14 days. A High
  dependency vulnerability is simultaneously governed by a 14-day and a 30-day SLA; Critical
  agrees at 7 days, which frames the divergence as drift, not design.
  Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.

### Medium

- **FR-202 (P2 + P5) TLS 1.2 exception vs corpus-wide TLS-1.3 prohibition.**
  `operations/procedure-media-handling-and-transport.md:124` permits TLS 1.2 "only where a
  documented technical constraint prevents TLS 1.3"; `security/policy-encryption-and-key-management.md:54`
  mandates "TLS 1.3 or stronger" with no exception; `operations/standard-production-security-requirements.md:184`
  requires "TLS 1.0, 1.1, and 1.2 must be disabled at the OS level". A device required to disable
  TLS 1.2 cannot invoke the media-handling carve-out. K (fresh carrier of closed FR-127); blame
  confirms the carrier predates the FR-127 fix. Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-203 (P7) certificate-key minimum below the crypto baseline.**
  `operations/standard-certificate-authority-management.md:105` sets a minimum of "2048-bit RSA
  or P-256 ECDSA", while `security/policy-encryption-and-key-management.md:90` and
  `security/framework-cryptographic-key-lifecycle.md:50` mandate RSA-4096 / ECC P-384, and
  `security/roadmap-post-quantum-cryptography.md:111` names RSA-2048 and P-256 as "legacy ... to
  retire" (with PKI at `:57` on the RSA-4096 baseline). The CA standard names the encryption
  policy in its Related Documents yet contradicts it. Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-204 (P7) password reuse and privileged-length divergence.**
  `security/procedure-identity-management.md:76` sets a blanket "Minimum 14 characters" and `:78`
  "No reuse of the last 10 passwords", while `security/standard-authentication-and-password-management.md:49`
  requires "20 characters for privileged accounts" and `:52` "the last 12 passwords must not be
  reused". Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-205 (P5) MFA scope drift policy-vs-standard.**
  `security/standard-authentication-and-password-management.md:60` mandates MFA "for all accounts"
  (and claims at `:17` to consolidate the IAM policy), while `security/policy-identity-and-access-management.md:60`
  scopes MFA to "all privileged accounts and remote access" and `security/policy-information-security.md:82`
  to "All privileged accounts". The consolidating standard is materially broader than the policies
  that authorize it. Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-206 (P9) DSR statutory-clock coverage gap.**
  `privacy/template-dsar-workflow.md:85` and `privacy/procedure-data-subject-rights-management.md:60`
  carry response clocks for GDPR (one month), CCPA/CPRA (45 days), and PIPEDA (30 days) only,
  while both name LGPD and PIPL in scope (`template-dsar-workflow.md:25`); the corpus's own annexes
  document tighter fixed windows the operational documents omit: Brazil 15-day confirmation/access
  (`privacy/jurisdictions/annex-privacy-brazil.md:38`, which explicitly defers its SLA to "their
  DSR procedure") and Mexico 20-day ARCO determination (`privacy/jurisdictions/annex-privacy-mexico.md:47`,
  Article 31). A fulfilment team running the GDPR one-month clock would miss both.
  Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-207 (P1 + P10) Board/CEO portal audience under-advertised.**
  The generated portal serves a tenth "Board / CEO" audience (`docs/portal.md:104`;
  `tools/build-portal.py:64` AUDIENCES), but the two hand-maintained README enumerations
  (`README.md:79`, `:217`) and the `tools/build-portal.py:6` module docstring still list only
  nine (CIO, CISO, GRC, Security Architecture, Privacy, Compliance, Audit, Resilience,
  Engineering). Multi-surface incompleteness: adding Board/CEO to the generator did not update the
  prose enumerations. Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-208 (P6) AI incident-response plan invokes an undefined "Joint Command".**
  `ai/plan-ai-incident-response.md:64` triggers "The Joint Command convenes for P1 within 60
  minutes of declaration" at the most time-critical point, but the term appears exactly once in
  the plan (grep count 1), is never defined, and the plan carries no roles/responsibilities
  section (headings run Purpose, Scope, classes, channels, Severity, Lifecycle 1-7, Coordination,
  Evidence, Framework alignment, Limitations). Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.
- **FR-209 (P8) implementation-roadmap misdirects to the wrong composition document.**
  `docs/template-implementation-roadmap.md:47` correctly says "Complete the startup-roadmap
  composition first" (the module catalogue lives only in `template-startup-roadmap.md`), but `:77`,
  `:260`, and `:273` all say "quickstart(-template) composition", which carries only the Day-1
  floor. Three carriers contradict the authoritative line 47; an adopter is misdirected by one
  hop. Verdict: confirmed-as-stated (three carriers, one more than P8 cited).
- **FR-210 (P3) internal-audit CAE fallback creates a self-review threat.**
  `compliance/standard-internal-audit.md:72` names the "GRC Manager" as CAE fallback, but
  `governance/register-role-authority.md:64` gives the GRC Programme Manager ownership of
  control-testing scheduling and the continuous-assurance frameworks, the very programme internal
  audit assures, contradicting the same standard's own `:93` auditor-independence rule; the title
  "GRC Manager" also diverges from the register's canonical "GRC Programme Manager".
  Verdict: confirmed-as-stated.

## Cross-Library Findings

- **Dominant pattern: operational/dedicated document restates an authoritative value that drifts.**
  FR-201 (SCA vs vuln-mgmt vs dev-req), FR-202 (media-handling vs encryption policy), FR-203 (CA
  standard vs crypto policy/framework), FR-204 and FR-205 (identity procedure/policies vs auth
  standard). The fix shape is uniform: point the restating document at the single authority, or
  state an explicit justified carve-out. The security / dev-security technical-control layer is
  where this class concentrates.
- **Convergences.** TLS 1.2 (P2 + P5); Board/CEO audience (P1 + P10); the value-restatement class
  (P5 + P7). Convergence raises confidence, not severity beyond the higher persona's call.
- **The corpus is genuinely strong elsewhere.** P4 verified the entire retention web consistent
  against eight source procedures with zero contradictions; P8 found every adoption path navigable
  with no dead-ends; P9 found the transfer ladders and breach-clock asymmetry coherent.

## Severity Model

High = must-fix-before-next-major-reliance-event; Medium = should-fix-this-review-cycle; Low =
routine cleanup; FYI = informational. In deep-assessment (trust-recovery-inheriting) mode the
triage governs the routing tier, not whether a finding is surfaced: every confirmed finding is
routed, none dropped, and the maintainer triages at r3 Phase-8 sign-off.

## Recommendations

1. Designate one authority per drifting value and point restating documents at it (FR-201 to
   FR-205); the enterprise vulnerability-management procedure and the encryption policy are the
   natural authorities for their classes.
2. Add the board / audit-committee functional line to the internal-audit standard (FR-200) and the
   CAE-fallback recusal control (FR-210); these are the highest-value doctrine fixes.
3. Close the DSR statutory-clock gap (FR-206), stricter-safe, by adding the LGPD 15-day and Mexico
   20-day clocks the annexes already document.
4. The clear-mechanical fixes (FR-207 README/generator enumeration; FR-209 three "quickstart"
   carriers to "startup-roadmap"; FR-213 stale INAI caveat) are low-risk and can be batched once
   the maintainer confirms.

## Standardization Recommendations

- A single-source-of-truth convention for cross-document control values (SLAs, key sizes, TLS
  floors, password parameters, MFA scope): the authoritative document states the value, restating
  documents reference it rather than copying it. This class recurs across fitness runs (FR-127,
  the r2 risk-scoring cluster) and is the strongest candidate for a structural convention.
- A bare-token corpus-wide grep at value-contradiction fix time (the FR-202/FR-127 lesson): a fix
  scoped to the carriers a finding named leaves un-named carriers live.
