## Purpose

This Charter establishes the governance framework, authority model, and accountability structure for the creation, approval, maintenance, and oversight of governance instruments across the enterprise. Governance instruments include frameworks, policies, standards, procedures, guidelines, registers, matrices, and related meta-governance artefacts.

The Charter ensures alignment with organizational strategy, regulatory obligations, and internationally recognized standards including ISO, NIST, COBIT 2025, and Cloud Security Alliance guidance. It defines the principles and structures necessary to support consistent, transparent, and effective governance.

---

## Scope

This Charter applies to all organizational units, subsidiaries, departments, third-party providers, and governance bodies operating under enterprise direction. It governs:

1. Creation, review, approval, maintenance, and retirement of governance instruments.  
2. Roles and responsibilities of document owners, contributors, reviewers, approvers, and custodians.  
3. Governance of meta-governance artefacts including regulatory mappings, compliance matrices, and digital-trust performance indicators.  
4. Integration of governance requirements across security, technology, privacy, AI, sustainability, compliance, and enterprise risk domains.  
5. Adoption of international trade-security frameworks where applicable, including WCO SAFE, ISO 28000, BASC, PIP, CTPAT, and AEO.

---

## Objectives

1. Establish a unified, transparent governance authority model.  
2. Ensure governance instruments comply with enterprise governance structure and quality criteria.  
3. Support consistent and traceable lifecycle management for all governance artefacts.  
4. Strengthen accountability for oversight, monitoring, and regulatory alignment.  
5. Enable continuous improvement and digital-trust outcomes.

---

## Governance and Accountability

### Governance Principles

1. **Integrity** – Decisions and actions reflect ethical, legal, and professional standards.  
2. **Transparency** – Governance decisions, changes, and approvals are documented and communicated consistently.  
3. **Continuous Improvement** – Governance practices evolve according to risk, technology, and regulatory developments.  
4. **Digital Trust** – Governance supports accountability, resilience, transparency, and responsible innovation.

---

### Governance Hierarchy

1. **Governance Charter** – Establishes foundational authority, structure, and decision rights.  
2. **Frameworks** – Provide strategic governance structure, domain integration, and regulatory alignment.  
3. **Policies** – Define executive intent and mandatory governance principles.  
4. **Standards** – Define measurable, enforceable control requirements.  
5. **Procedures** – Specify operational steps to implement standards.  
6. **Guidelines** – Provide advisory or optional methods where flexibility is permitted.  
7. **Registers and Matrices** – Consolidate inventories, mappings, metrics, evidence, and cross-framework relationships.

---

### Governance Structure and Oversight

1. **Chief Executive Officer** – Holds ultimate accountability for governance effectiveness.  
2. **Chief Governance Officer** – Oversees the GRC library and governance architecture.  
3. **Chief Information Security Officer** – Oversees information-security and resilience governance.  
4. **Chief Compliance Officer** – Leads compliance governance and regulatory monitoring.  
5. **Chief Legal Officer** – Provides regulatory interpretation and legal oversight.  
6. **Chief Risk Officer** – Oversees enterprise-risk governance, appetite, and monitoring.  
7. **Chief Technology Officer** – Governs technology architecture, innovation, and control assurance.  
8. **Chief Sustainability Officer** – Integrates ESG and responsible-technology governance.  
9. **Chief Human Resources Officer** – Oversees ethics, conduct, and workforce governance.  
10. **Enterprise Risk Committee** – Provides senior oversight of enterprise risk, audit results, and compliance outcomes.

---

## Policy and Control Statements

1. Governance artefacts must align with organizational strategy, risk appetite, and recognized global frameworks.  
2. All governance artefacts must declare parent documents, related frameworks, and dependent artefacts.  
3. Change management requirements apply to all creation, updates, and retirement activities.  
4. All governance artefacts must be stored in the authoritative GRC repository.  
5. Meta-governance artefacts (mappings, registers, matrices) must be maintained as separate documents and referenced accordingly.  
6. Version control must ensure traceability, archival of superseded versions, and transparent revision history.  
7. Governance artefacts must incorporate global supply-chain and trade-security requirements when applicable.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

1. **Governance Owners** – Maintain applicability, accuracy, and currency of assigned artefacts.  
2. **Approving Authorities** – Validate and approve governance artefacts per defined workflows.  
3. **Contributors and Reviewers** – Ensure clarity, quality, and alignment during development and updates.  
4. **Enterprise Risk Committee** – Receives consolidated reporting on governance, compliance, and assurance outcomes.  
5. **All Personnel** – Must follow governance instruments and report gaps, risks, or improvement opportunities.

---

## Monitoring, Metrics, and Reporting

1. Governance effectiveness is assessed through audits, maturity assessments, performance metrics, and digital-trust indicators.  
2. Risk, compliance, and performance indicators are reviewed regularly by oversight committees.  
3. Deviations or exceptions must be documented and processed through the exception-management workflow.

---

## Continuous Improvement

1. Governance instruments must be reviewed annually or when significant regulatory, technological, or strategic changes occur.  
2. Audit findings, incident reviews, and lessons learned must inform updates.  
3. Non-substantive editorial changes may be made without re-approval but must be logged for traceability.
