Purpose
This framework defines the structure, hierarchy, and interrelationships governing all artefacts within the GRC documentation corpus. It establishes an authoritative model for document typology, dependency mapping, control flow, assurance integration, and version governance, ensuring that frameworks, policies, standards, procedures, and supporting artefacts operate coherently under a unified governance architecture.

Scope
This framework applies to all documents within the GRC library and all future documents created under governance authority. It governs document purpose, hierarchical positioning, upstream and downstream dependencies, lifecycle expectations, and integration with assurance, regulatory, and performance frameworks.

Objectives
- Define the canonical document hierarchy used across the enterprise.
- Establish a consistent classification and dependency framework.
- Enable top-down governance flow and bottom-up assurance integration.
- Provide authoritative relationships between all GRC artefact types.
- Support automation for mapping, indexing, routing, and evidence alignment.

Governance and Accountability
The Chief Compliance Officer maintains ownership of this framework. The Chief Risk Officer serves as the approving authority. All document owners across governance, risk, compliance, privacy, security, AI, sustainability, and IT domains must ensure their artefacts align to this framework. Internal Audit validates adherence during assurance activities.

Methodology: Document Typology and Hierarchy
The GRC documentation system consists of the following standard artefact types:

Framework
Defines strategic intent, governance structure, scope, and integration model. Provides top-level authority and alignment with external frameworks and regulatory obligations.

Policy
Defines binding obligations, executive intent, mandatory principles, and high-level control expectations. All policies must derive authority from one or more frameworks.

Standard
Defines specific, measurable, and enforceable control requirements. Implements policy intent through operationalized technical or procedural expectations.

Procedure
Defines structured, step-by-step processes enabling consistent execution of standards or policies.

Guideline
Provides advisory, interpretive, or best-practice guidance supporting standards and procedures.

Plan
Defines tactical, coordinated actions for incident response, continuity, recovery, and organizational resilience.

Charter
Establishes the mandate, authority, scope, and responsibilities of governance bodies or committees.

Register
Consolidates inventories, metrics, mappings, decisions, or evidence required for audit and assurance.

Matrix
Provides structured mapping between controls, frameworks, lifecycle phases, or regulatory requirements.

Specification / Annex / Checklist
Provides supplemental or region-specific requirements, detailed instructions, or validation criteria supporting higher-level artefacts.

Governance and Dependency Structure
Document relationships follow a strict hierarchical flow:

Frameworks → Policies → Standards → Procedures → Plans
↑                                            ↓
└─────────────── Assurance, Metrics, Audit ───────────────┘

Top-Down Governance Flow
- Frameworks establish authority, boundaries, and strategic direction.
- Policies derive from frameworks and define binding control intent.
- Standards implement policy requirements with measurable criteria.
- Procedures operationalize standards through repeatable actions.
- Plans provide actionable execution paths during events or contingencies.

Bottom-Up Assurance Integration
- Evidence produced through procedures, controls, monitoring, and testing flows upward to inform assurance, performance, and regulatory reporting.
- Registers and matrices consolidate outputs for external audits, internal assurance, and continuous improvement governance.

Dependency and Interrelationship Model
The following rules apply to all document relationships:

- Each document must declare parent document(s) that grant authority.
- Each document must declare dependent or downstream artefacts that rely on it.
- New artefacts must identify regulatory frameworks and external standards they support.
- Only frameworks may serve as primary sources of governance authority.
- Policies may not derive from other policies unless explicitly scoped.
- Standards must map to at least one policy.
- Procedures must map to one or more standards.
- Registers and matrices must specify their coverage domain and supporting artefacts.

Monitoring, Metrics, and Reporting
Adherence to this framework is monitored through:
- The master index and repository governance structure.
- Cross-framework and regulatory mappings.
- Periodic quality reviews performed by governance and compliance functions.
- Audit validations confirming alignment with the documented hierarchy.

Continuous Improvement
The document hierarchy, dependency model, and classification taxonomy must be reviewed annually. Findings from audits, maturity assessments, and incident reviews must be incorporated into updates. All substantive changes must be logged in the Continuous Improvement Register.
