## Purpose

This policy establishes a reusable baseline for managing compliance obligations, audit planning, control testing, corrective action, and assurance reporting.

---

## Policy Statements

1. Compliance obligations must be identified, assessed for applicability, assigned to accountable roles, and mapped to controls or evidence classes.
2. Audit and assurance activities must be risk-based and must include scope, criteria, evidence requirements, independence expectations, and reporting method.
3. Control testing must evaluate design and operating effectiveness where evidence is available.
4. Audit findings must be classified by severity, owner, remediation target, evidence requirement, and closure criteria.
5. Corrective and preventive actions must be tracked to closure and reviewed for recurrence.
6. Regulatory applicability must distinguish legal obligation, regulatory interpretation, contractual requirement, industry practice, and architectural recommendation.
7. Assurance reporting must avoid unsupported compliance claims. Evidence, scope, and limitations must be explicit.
8. AI, privacy, supplier, cloud, and resilience controls must be included in audit scope where they materially affect obligations or risk.

---

## Minimum Evidence

Adopting organizations should maintain obligation registers, control matrices, audit plans, test workpapers, evidence records, findings, remediation plans, closure approvals, and management reports.

---

## Limitations

This policy does not establish compliance with any law, regulation, certification, or contract. Compliance depends on applicability, implemented controls, evidence, legal review, and operating effectiveness.
