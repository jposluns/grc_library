## Purpose

This policy establishes a reusable governance and risk management baseline for identifying, assessing, treating, monitoring, accepting, and reporting risk across business, technology, security, privacy, AI, supplier, compliance, and resilience domains.

---

## Policy Statements

1. Governance instruments must define role-based accountability, authority, review cadence, and evidence expectations.
2. Risks must be recorded in a risk register when they may materially affect confidentiality, integrity, availability, privacy, safety, compliance, operations, finance, reputation, resilience, supplier dependency, or AI system outcomes.
3. Risk assessment must consider likelihood, impact, control effectiveness, residual exposure, and decision authority.
4. Risk treatment must be documented as mitigation, transfer, avoidance, acceptance, or further analysis.
5. Risk acceptance must have an accountable owner, defined rationale, expiry or review date, compensating controls where applicable, and approval proportionate to severity.
6. Exceptions to policies, standards, procedures, or controls must follow the exception process and must not be treated as undocumented operational convenience.
7. Risk information must be aggregated for governance reporting and used to prioritize remediation.
8. AI, privacy, supplier, cloud, and resilience risks must be assessed across data lifecycle, service dependency, control boundary, and residual risk.
9. Legal obligation, regulatory interpretation, contractual requirement, industry practice, and architectural recommendation must be distinguished in risk decisions.
10. Governance metrics must be evidence-based and must not substitute activity counts for operating effectiveness.

---

## Evidence Expectations

Adopting organizations should maintain risk registers, risk assessments, control assessments, treatment plans, exception records, acceptance records, review minutes, and remediation evidence.

---

## Limitations

This policy is a public-domain reference. It does not define an adopting organization's risk appetite, legal obligations, regulatory exposure, or control operating effectiveness.
