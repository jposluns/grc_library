## Purpose

This policy defines requirements for requesting, reviewing, approving, monitoring, and closing exceptions to approved governance requirements, policies, standards, procedures, controls, contractual requirements, or architectural baselines.

---

## Policy Statements

1. Exceptions must be documented before a deviation is implemented, unless emergency conditions require immediate containment or continuity action.
2. Exceptions must identify the requirement being bypassed, the reason for deviation, affected systems or processes, risk impact, compensating controls, duration, owner, and approval authority.
3. Exceptions must be time-bound and reviewed before expiry.
4. Permanent exceptions are prohibited unless converted into an approved change to the governing requirement.
5. High-risk or critical exceptions must require executive risk acceptance and independent security, privacy, compliance, or legal review where applicable.
6. Exceptions affecting AI systems must assess data exposure, prompt injection risk, data poisoning risk, model inversion risk, membership inference risk, unsafe tool use, monitoring limitations, and deletion feasibility where relevant.
7. Exceptions affecting suppliers must assess contractual commitments, data handling, service continuity, subprocessor exposure, breach notification, and exit capability.
8. Expired exceptions must be remediated, renewed with justification, or escalated as unresolved risk.

---

## Minimum Exception Record

An exception record must include:

- Exception ID.
- Requesting role.
- Accountable owner.
- Requirement or control affected.
- Business justification.
- Risk assessment.
- Compensating controls.
- Start date.
- Expiry date.
- Approval authority.
- Review cadence.
- Closure criteria.
- Evidence references.

---

## Limitations

An exception is not a silent waiver, a governance amnesty programme, or a way to convert poor control design into a spreadsheet. It is a documented, risk-owned, time-bound deviation.
