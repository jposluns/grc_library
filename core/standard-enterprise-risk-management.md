## Purpose

This standard defines minimum requirements for risk identification, analysis, evaluation, treatment, monitoring, and reporting. It provides a reusable risk-management baseline that can be adapted to any organization.

---

## Requirements

### 1. Risk Taxonomy

Organizations should maintain a risk taxonomy covering at least:

- Strategic risk.
- Operational risk.
- Cybersecurity risk.
- Privacy and data protection risk.
- AI system risk.
- Supplier and third-party risk.
- Resilience and continuity risk.
- Legal and regulatory risk.
- Financial risk.
- Technology and architecture risk.
- Human capital and insider risk.
- Physical, environmental, and facility risk.

### 2. Risk Assessment

Each material risk assessment must record:

- Risk statement.
- Cause.
- Event.
- Impact.
- Affected assets, services, data, suppliers, or AI systems.
- Inherent likelihood and impact.
- Existing controls.
- Control effectiveness.
- Residual likelihood and impact.
- Treatment decision.
- Owner.
- Review date.
- Evidence references.

### 3. Risk Scoring

Risk scoring must be consistently applied. The scoring model should define scale, criteria, aggregation logic, and escalation thresholds. Qualitative ratings must be supported by rationale and evidence.

### 4. Treatment

Treatment decisions must be documented as mitigation, transfer, avoidance, acceptance, monitoring, or further analysis. Treatment plans must include owner, target date, required action, dependency, expected residual risk, and status.

### 5. Risk Acceptance

Risk acceptance must be time-bound, owner-approved, evidence-supported, and proportionate to severity. High or critical residual risk should require executive or governing-body approval.

### 6. Monitoring and Reporting

Risk reporting must identify open risks, risk trend, overdue treatments, material changes, high-risk exceptions, control failures, supplier exposure, AI risk exposure, and resilience risk.

---

## Evidence Requirements

Required evidence may include risk register entries, assessments, treatment plans, control tests, exception approvals, meeting records, supplier assessments, AI impact assessments, business impact analyses, and audit findings.
