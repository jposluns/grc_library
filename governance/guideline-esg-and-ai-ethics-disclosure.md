## Purpose

This guideline defines the organization's approach to disclosing Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) performance and AI ethics commitments to external stakeholders. It provides direction on what to disclose, how to disclose it, and which standards and frameworks govern disclosure content.

---

## Scope

Applies to all external ESG and AI ethics disclosures including: annual sustainability reports, investor communications, regulatory filings, supplier questionnaires, customer trust documentation, and public commitments.

---

## ESG disclosure principles

### 1. Materiality

Disclosures focus on matters that are material to stakeholders: those that could reasonably influence their decisions. The organization applies a double materiality lens: impact on society and environment (impact materiality) and financial implications (financial materiality).

### 2. Accuracy and verifiability

Disclosures are based on measured data where possible. Estimates are clearly labelled as estimates with methodology disclosed. Data supporting disclosures is retained per the Data Retention Schedule for audit and verification purposes.

### 3. Completeness

Disclosures cover all significant ESG dimensions: environmental performance, social practices (including human rights and workforce wellbeing), and governance effectiveness (including AI governance).

### 4. Comparability

Disclosures use standardized frameworks (GRI, TCFD, SASB) to enable stakeholder comparison across periods and peers.

## Disclosure standards and frameworks

The organization aligns its ESG and AI ethics disclosures with the following standards:

| Standard / Framework | Application |
| --- | --- |
| GRI Standards | Core environmental, social, and governance disclosures |
| TCFD Recommendations | Climate-related financial risk disclosures |
| SASB Standards | Industry-specific sustainability disclosures (where applicable) |
| OECD AI Principles | AI ethics commitments and transparency |
| EU AI Act | High-risk AI system disclosures to competent authorities |
| GPAI Model Governance Playbook | AI model governance disclosures |
| ISO/IEC 42001 | AI management system disclosures |

---

## Publication and review

The annual ESG and AI Ethics Report is:
- Reviewed by the ERC before publication.
- Published on the organization's external website.
- Aligned with the annual governance review cycle.
- Retained in the governance document repository per the Data Retention Schedule.
