## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for capturing, tracking, and managing improvement initiatives across all governance domains through the Continuous Improvement Register (CIR). The CIR is the organization's central record of proactive improvement actions, distinct from reactive CAPA actions, and supports the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) continual improvement cycle across GRC, security, compliance, and operational domains.

---

## Scope

Applies to all improvement initiatives arising from: management reviews, audit findings (observation-level), lessons learned, staff suggestions, benchmarking, and strategic development activities. The CIR captures proactive improvements; reactive corrective actions are managed in the CAPA Register.

---

## Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **GRC Programme Manager** | Owns and maintains the CIR; reports progress to the ERC quarterly. |
| **Domain Owners** | Identify and submit improvement ideas; own assigned improvement actions. |
| **Internal Audit** | Reviews CIR completeness and quality as part of annual governance review. |
| **ERC** | Reviews CIR progress quarterly; approves resource-intensive improvements. |

---

## 1. Improvement identification

Improvement ideas may be submitted by any employee, contractor, or governance body. Common improvement sources include:

- Post-incident reviews and lessons learned.
- Internal and external audit observations (non-finding level).
- Management review outcomes.
- Regulatory or framework updates requiring proactive adaptation.
- Employee suggestions or security awareness feedback.
- Benchmarking against industry standards or peers.
- AI governance maturity assessments.

---

## 2. Improvement registration

Each improvement initiative is logged in the Continuous Improvement Register with:

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| CIR ID | Unique identifier (format: CIR-YYYY-NNN) |
| Title | Short descriptive title |
| Description | What improvement is proposed and why |
| Source | Audit observation / incident lesson / management review / other |
| Domain | Governance / Security / Compliance / AI / Operations / Privacy / etc. |
| Priority | High / Medium / Low (see criteria below) |
| Owner | Role title of responsible party |
| Target Date | Expected completion date |
| Resources Required | Estimated effort or budget (if applicable) |
| Status | Proposed / Approved / In Progress / Complete / Deferred |
| Completion Evidence | Description of evidence confirming improvement |
| Closed Date | Date improvement verified as complete |

**Priority criteria:**

| Priority | Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| High | Directly reduces a High or Critical risk; required for certification or regulatory compliance |
| Medium | Reduces a Medium risk; improves governance maturity or efficiency |
| Low | Minor process enhancement; quality-of-life improvement |

---

## 3. Review and approval

3.1 The GRC Programme Manager reviews submitted improvements weekly and updates status.

3.2 High-priority improvements are reviewed and approved by the relevant domain head within 5 business days.

3.3 Improvements requiring significant resource investment (budget, headcount, or major process change) are presented to the ERC for approval.

3.4 Deferred improvements are documented with a rationale and a target re-review date.

---

## 4. Progress tracking and reporting

4.1 The GRC Programme Manager produces a CIR progress summary quarterly for the ERC.

4.2 The quarterly summary includes:
- Total open improvements by domain and priority.
- Improvements completed in the quarter.
- Overdue improvements with explanation.
- Newly submitted improvements.

4.3 The annual CIR summary is included in the GRC Annual Review Report.

---

## 5. Closure and verification

5.1 Improvements are closed only when the domain owner provides completion evidence.

5.2 High-priority improvements are verified by the GRC Programme Manager or Internal Audit before closure.

5.3 Closed improvements are retained in the register for audit purposes per the Data Retention Schedule.

---

## Relationship to CAPA register

The CIR and CAPA Register are complementary:

| Register | Purpose | Trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Continuous Improvement Register (CIR)** | Proactive improvement; good-practice advancement | Management review, observations, lessons learned, strategic initiatives |
| **CAPA Register** | Reactive corrective and preventive action | Audit nonconformities, incidents, control failures, regulatory findings |

Improvement actions arising from audit observations (non-nonconformity) are recorded in the CIR. Nonconformities and material control failures are recorded in the CAPA Register.
