## Purpose

This procedure defines the channels, protections, investigation processes, and reporting obligations for whistleblower disclosures and internal incident reporting. It ensures that concerns about misconduct, legal violations, ethical breaches, security incidents, or regulatory non-compliance are reported safely, investigated thoroughly, and resolved appropriately.

---

## Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and third parties who access organisational systems or resources and who have concerns about:
- Ethical violations or misconduct.
- Legal or regulatory breaches.
- Safety or security incidents.
- Financial irregularities or fraud.
- Bribery or corruption.
- Data protection violations.
- AI ethics concerns.

## Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Owns whistleblower programme; receives and manages disclosures. |
| **Ethics and Compliance Committee (ECC)** | Reviews whistleblower reports; approves investigation outcomes. |
| **HR / People Operations** | Manages employment-related disciplinary outcomes. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advises on legal obligations; manages regulatory notifications. |
| **CISO** | Investigates security-related disclosures. |
| **Internal Audit** | Independently reviews whistleblower programme effectiveness annually. |

## 1. Reporting channels

Disclosures may be submitted through any of the following channels:

| Channel | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Direct Manager** | Appropriate for routine concerns without conflict of interest |
| **HR / People Operations** | Appropriate for employment, conduct, or HR-related concerns |
| **Chief Compliance Officer** | Appropriate for compliance, legal, or ethical concerns |
| **Anonymous Reporting Channel** | Available for sensitive disclosures; managed by the ECC |
| **External Regulator** | Available where the reporter believes internal channels are inadequate or compromised |

Anonymous disclosures are accepted and investigated to the extent possible given available information. Reporters are encouraged to provide sufficient detail to enable investigation.

---

## 2. Whistleblower protections

The organisation prohibits any retaliation against individuals who make a disclosure in good faith. Protections include:

- Employment protection: no dismissal, demotion, suspension, or unfavourable treatment.
- Confidentiality: reporter identity is protected to the maximum extent possible.
- Support: reporters may request access to support services during the investigation period.

Retaliation against a whistleblower is itself a disciplinary offence subject to the Security Disciplinary Process.

Good faith is defined as a genuine belief that the reported concern may be true. Malicious or knowingly false reports are not protected.

---

## 3. Incident reporting

All staff are required to report actual or suspected:
- Information security incidents (data breaches, malware, phishing, unauthorized access).
- Privacy breaches or data loss events.
- Physical security incidents.
- AI ethics or safety concerns.
- Health and safety incidents.

Reports are made to the relevant team lead, IT/security helpdesk, or the CISO as appropriate for the incident type. Security incident reporting follows the Security Incident Reporting and Escalation Procedure.

---

## 4. Investigation process

4.1 The Chief Compliance Officer acknowledges receipt of a disclosure within 5 business days.

4.2 A preliminary assessment determines:
- Whether the disclosure is within scope.
- Whether it requires immediate action (e.g., imminent legal risk, active breach).
- Whether it requires investigation by Internal Audit, Legal, or an external party.

4.3 An investigation is conducted proportionate to the severity of the concern:
- Minor concerns: resolved through conversation or management action.
- Significant concerns: formal investigation with documented findings.
- Criminal or regulatory matters: referred to Legal Counsel for guidance on external reporting.

4.4 The reporter is informed of the outcome to the extent permitted by confidentiality and legal constraints.

4.5 Investigations are documented and retained per the Data Retention Schedule.

---

## 5. Regulatory notification obligations

Where an investigation confirms a breach that may trigger regulatory notification obligations, Legal Counsel assesses:

| Regulation | Notification Obligation |
| --- | --- |
| GDPR | Notify supervisory authority within 72 hours of confirmed personal data breach |
| PIPEDA / Quebec Law 25 | Notify OPC and affected individuals for significant risk breaches |
| UK GDPR | Notify ICO within 72 hours |
| LGPD | Notify ANPD within a reasonable timeframe |
| EU AI Act | Notify competent authority for serious incidents involving High-risk AI systems |
| NIS 2 Directive | Notify competent authority within 24 hours for significant incidents |

---

## 6. Programme effectiveness review

The Chief Compliance Officer reviews the whistleblower programme annually and reports to the ERC on:
- Number and category of disclosures received.
- Investigation outcomes.
- Programme awareness levels.
- Any identified programme improvements.
