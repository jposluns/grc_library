## Purpose

This annex defines privacy and AI regulatory requirements applicable to the processing of personal data in Malaysia under the Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (Act 709) as amended in 2024. It supplements the Privacy and Data Governance Policy and the Privacy Impact and Cross-Border Transfer Procedure.

---

## Applicable laws and regulatory authorities

- **Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (Act 709)**: As amended by the **Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Act 2024 (Act A1727)**. The Amendment Act commences in three tranches: tranche 1 from 1 January 2025 (which introduced the renaming of the data-subject role and other foundational changes); tranche 2 from 1 April 2025; and tranche 3 from 1 June 2025 (including the mandatory DPO appointment, the 72-hour breach notification obligation, and the right to data portability).
- Seven data protection principles: General; Notice and Choice; Disclosure; Security; Retention; Data Integrity; Access.
- **2024 amendments introduced:**
  - **Mandatory DPO (from 1 June 2025):** Both data controllers and data processors must appoint at least one DPO. The DPO must either be resident in Malaysia (physically present in Malaysia for at least 180 days in one calendar year) or easily contactable by other means. Per the Guidelines on the Appointment of a Data Protection Officer issued by the Personal Data Protection Commissioner on 25 February 2025.
  - **Breach notification (from 1 June 2025):** Data controllers must notify the Personal Data Protection Commissioner as soon as practicable, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware, of a personal data breach. Where the breach poses a risk of significant harm to data subjects, affected individuals must be notified within 7 days. Per the Guidelines on Data Breach Notification issued 25 February 2025.
  - **Right to data portability**.
  - **Direct accountability of data processors** (previously only controllers were accountable).
  - **Increased penalties** (see Enforcement and fines below).
- **Regulatory authority:** Personal Data Protection Commissioner under the Ministry of Digital. The Personal Data Protection Department (JPDP) administers the Act and issues guidelines.

---

## AI and privacy obligations

- The seven data protection principles apply to AI systems processing personal data, including transparency (Notice and Choice), purpose limitation (Disclosure), and security requirements.
- A national AI Ethics Principle framework (2021) provides voluntary guidance for AI deployments.
- The Personal Data Protection Commissioner has confirmed that AI systems processing personal data must comply with Act 709 principles.

---

## Cross-border transfer mechanisms

- Cross-border transfers permitted only to countries approved by the Minister of Digital (approved country list), or by satisfying one of the alternative grounds set out in section 129 of the Act as amended.
- Where the recipient country is not on the approved list: explicit consent of the data subject or other specified exceptions are required.
- The Personal Data Protection Department issued Cross-Border Transfer Guidelines in mid-2025 setting out the practical requirements for relying on the alternative grounds (Transfer Impact Assessment, contractual safeguards, and similar).

---

## Enforcement and fines

- Administrative fines up to MYR 1 million per offence (increased by the 2024 amendment).
- Imprisonment up to 3 years for serious violations (2024 amendment).
