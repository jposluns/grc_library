## Purpose

This annex defines privacy and AI regulatory requirements applicable to the processing of personal data in Vietnam under Decree No. 13/2023/ND-CP on Personal Data Protection. It supplements the Privacy and Data Governance Policy and the Privacy Impact and Cross-Border Transfer Procedure.

---

## Applicable laws and regulatory authorities

- **Decree No. 13/2023/ND-CP on Personal Data Protection**: Effective 2023-07-01. Vietnam's first dedicated personal data protection regulation.
- Classifies personal data as basic and sensitive. Sensitive categories include: political views, religion, health, genetic, biometric, financial, location, and nationality data.
- Consent required for most processing. Sensitive personal data of Vietnamese citizens must be stored within Vietnam for a minimum period; transfers abroad require consent and meeting additional conditions.
- Mandatory breach notification within 72 hours to Department A05.
- **Regulatory authority:** Ministry of Public Security (Department A05) with sector-specific oversight by other ministries.

---

## AI and privacy obligations

- Disclosure of automated processing used for decision-making about individuals is required.
- Vietnam's broader AI regulatory framework is under development as of 2025.
- Purpose limitation and consent requirements apply to personal data used in AI systems.

---

## Cross-border transfer mechanisms

- Transfers require consent plus meeting one of: equivalent protection in the recipient jurisdiction; a bilateral agreement; or specific approval.
- Sensitive personal data of Vietnamese citizens is subject to in-country storage requirements for a minimum retention period before transfer.

---

## Enforcement and fines

- Administrative fines under the cybercrime and data protection framework.
- Criminal prosecution for serious violations.
- Decree 13/2023 enforcement matured through 2025 with increasing regulatory activity from Department A05.

## Limitations

This document is a public-domain reference baseline. It does not constitute legal advice. Regulatory frameworks change frequently; verify currency before reliance.
