## Purpose

This policy defines organization-neutral privacy and data governance principles for lawful, fair, secure, transparent, and accountable handling of personal data, regulated data, confidential data, and data used by AI systems.

The policy provides a reusable baseline. Adopting organizations must validate jurisdiction-specific obligations, sector requirements, contractual commitments, data residency, and processing roles.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to data collection, creation, receipt, classification, storage, access, sharing, transfer, processing, analytics, AI use, monitoring, retention, deletion, archival, and disposal.

It applies to:

- Personal data.
- Sensitive personal data.
- Employee data.
- Customer data.
- Supplier data.
- Confidential business data.
- Regulated data.
- AI training, fine-tuning, prompt, retrieval, inference, output, monitoring, and evaluation data.

---

## Policy Requirements

### 1. Lawful and Authorized Processing

Data must be processed only for defined, authorized, and documented purposes. Where legal basis, contractual authority, consent, notice, or legitimate interest analysis is required, it must be documented before processing.

### 2. Data Minimization

Only the minimum data required for an approved purpose should be collected, retained, accessed, shared, or used. AI systems must not receive broader data access merely because the integration is technically convenient.

### 3. Data Classification

Data must be classified according to sensitivity, regulatory status, business criticality, confidentiality, integrity, availability, and lifecycle obligations.

### 4. Data Inventory and Lineage

Data inventories must identify data owners, systems, processing purposes, data categories, recipients, suppliers, retention periods, transfer conditions, and deletion methods. AI systems must maintain data provenance and lineage across training, retrieval, inference, monitoring, and retention stages.

### 5. Access Control

Access to data must be approved, role-appropriate, logged where required, reviewed periodically, and removed when no longer needed.

### 6. Cross-Border Transfers

Cross-border transfers must be assessed where personal data, regulated data, customer data, or contractually restricted data moves between jurisdictions or is accessed from another jurisdiction.

### 7. Supplier and Processor Governance

Suppliers that process, host, access, store, analyze, or transmit data must be assessed before use. Contracts should address confidentiality, security, subprocessors, breach notification, data residency, deletion, audit support, and exit.

### 8. AI Data Use

AI systems must not use personal, sensitive, confidential, or regulated data for training, fine-tuning, external model improvement, retrieval, prompt processing, or monitoring unless authorized and assessed. Submitted prompts, files, outputs, logs, and embeddings must be governed as data records.

### 9. Data Subject and Individual Rights

Where applicable, processes must exist to handle access, correction, deletion, objection, portability, restriction, consent withdrawal, and automated decision-related requests.

### 10. Retention and Deletion

Data must have defined retention and deletion rules. Deletion must address primary data, backups where feasible, logs, derived datasets, embeddings, retrieval stores, AI monitoring records, supplier-held records, and archived records.

### 11. Privacy and Data Protection Impact Assessment

Impact assessments must be performed for high-risk processing, sensitive data, new technologies, AI systems, material supplier changes, cross-border transfers, monitoring, profiling, or processing that may materially affect individuals.

### 12. Incident and Breach Response

Suspected unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, loss, misuse, retention failure, deletion failure, or AI data leakage must be assessed through incident response and breach response processes.

## Evidence Requirements

Adopting organizations should maintain evidence such as:

- Data inventory.
- Processing activity register.
- Data classification record.
- Privacy impact assessment.
- Transfer assessment.
- Supplier assessment.
- Access review.
- Retention schedule.
- Deletion record.
- Breach assessment.
- AI data lineage record.
- Exception approval.

---

## Limitations

This policy does not determine whether a specific law applies. Applicability depends on jurisdiction, sector, establishment, data category, processing role, contractual terms, and operational facts.
