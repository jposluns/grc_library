## Purpose

This template defines the structure of a privacy notice that satisfies the information duties of GDPR Articles 13 and 14, UK GDPR equivalents, LGPD, PIPEDA, CPPA, PIPL, CCPA, and similar regimes. It accommodates both website-style standing notices and just-in-time notices delivered at the point of collection. Adopting organisations populate the placeholders, validate jurisdictional coverage, and publish through the appropriate channel.

A populated privacy notice is a public-facing document specific to an organisation; this template intentionally contains no organisation-specific content.

---

## Scope

This template applies wherever the organisation, acting as a controller, collects or otherwise processes personal data from a data subject. It covers data collected directly from the subject (Article 13) and data collected from other sources (Article 14). It does not replace processor-specific transparency obligations under data processing agreements.

---

## Required content blocks

Each privacy notice instantiated from this template must contain the following blocks. The order may be adjusted for readability but no block may be omitted unless the omission is documented and justified.

### 1. Identity of the controller

| Field | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Legal name of the controller | The organisation's legal entity name |
| Postal address | Registered office or other publishable contact address |
| Privacy contact | Privacy-team contact route; commonly an email and a postal address |
| Data Protection Officer (where appointed) | DPO contact route or statement that no DPO is appointed |
| EU representative (where the controller is non-EU but subject to GDPR) | Representative name and contact |
| UK representative (where the controller is non-UK but subject to UK GDPR) | Representative name and contact |
| Other jurisdictional representatives | As required by applicable laws |

### 2. Purposes of processing and lawful basis

| Field | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Purposes | Each purpose for which personal data is processed |
| Lawful basis | Per jurisdiction; GDPR Article 6 reference and special-category condition under Article 9 where applicable |
| Legitimate interest balancing | Where consent is not relied on for a given purpose under GDPR, a high-level statement of the legitimate interest and the rights balancing test |

### 3. Categories of personal data

State each category of personal data collected (identifying, contact, demographic, behavioural, financial, location, biometric, health, etc.). Identify special-category data separately. State whether providing the data is statutory, contractual, or voluntary, and what happens if the subject does not provide it.

### 4. Sources of data

Where data was collected from sources other than the subject (Article 14), state the source category (e.g. credit reference agency, public records, partner organisation, supplier) and whether the source was public.

### 5. Recipients

| Field | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Internal recipients | Functions or roles, not individual employee names |
| Processors | Categories of processors (e.g. cloud platform provider, customer support platform, analytics provider); link to the subprocessor register where adopted |
| Other controllers | Joint controllers or independent controllers where the data is shared |
| Government and regulatory recipients | Where disclosure is or may be required by law |

### 6. Transfers outside the local jurisdiction

| Field | Required content |
| --- | --- |
| Destination countries | Country list per transfer |
| Transfer mechanism | Adequacy decision; Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs); UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA); Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs); derogation under Article 49 |
| Safeguards summary | Plain-language safeguards relied upon |
| Right to receive a copy of safeguards | Statement of the right and the channel to request the safeguard documentation |

### 7. Retention

State the retention period for each purpose, or the criteria used to determine retention. Reference the data retention schedule as the operational source of truth.

### 8. Data subject rights

State all rights that apply in each jurisdiction the notice covers. Default minimum set under GDPR / UK GDPR:

- Right of access
- Right to rectification
- Right to erasure
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object
- Rights related to automated decision-making and profiling
- Right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority
- Right to withdraw consent at any time where consent is the lawful basis

For each right, state the channel for exercising the right and the response window. Reference the data subject rights management procedure.

### 9. Automated decision-making and profiling

Where Article 22 applies, state the existence of automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effect, the logic involved at a high level, the significance and envisaged consequences, and the right to human review. Cross-reference the automated decision-making register.

### 10. Children's data

Where the controller knowingly collects personal data from children below the age of digital consent in any applicable jurisdiction, state this clearly. Identify the age threshold, the consent mechanism (e.g. verifiable parental consent), and the special protections applied. Cross-reference the children's data framework.

### 11. Cookies and similar tracking technologies

Where the notice is published on a website or mobile application using cookies or similar technologies, reference (or include inline) the cookie and tracker register. State the categories used, purposes, lawful basis, retention, and how consent or objection is managed.

### 12. Changes to this notice

State the date of last update, the version, and how subjects are notified of material changes (in-product banner, email, formal communication, etc.).

## Plain-language drafting requirements

1. The notice is written in plain language suitable for the intended audience. Where the intended audience includes children, the language is age-appropriate.
2. Sentence length is short; paragraphs are short; headings are descriptive.
3. Defined terms appear with a short definition the first time they are used.
4. Numbers and dates use clear local formats; legal citations include enough context for a reader to follow.
5. The notice is offered in every language required by applicable law or by the channel through which the data is collected.

---

## Just-in-time notice variant

For just-in-time notices delivered at the point of collection (form submission, sensor activation, biometric capture, AI interaction), an abbreviated subset is acceptable provided it contains: controller identity, purposes, lawful basis, retention summary, link to the full notice, and the rights summary or a link to it.

---

## Operating expectations

1. The privacy notice is published before any data is collected and is accessible through every collection channel.
2. Material changes follow the change-notification path declared in §12.
3. Translations are version-locked to the source language.
4. The notice is reviewed annually and upon every material change to processing, jurisdiction, supplier, or product.
5. The Privacy Officer maintains a change log including version, date, jurisdictions affected, and brief description.

---

## Limitations

This template is a CC BY-SA 4.0 baseline. Adopting organisations must validate jurisdictional coverage, lawful basis selections, and the rights framework against their specific operating jurisdictions. Where the controller relies on legitimate interest or carries out automated decision-making, additional legal-counsel review is required before publication. The template is not legal advice.
