## Purpose

This guideline provides organization-neutral guidance for immediate protective actions during emergencies that may affect personnel safety, facilities, operations, technology services, data, suppliers, or continuity response.

---

## Scope

This guideline may be adapted for offices, facilities, warehouses, operational sites, remote work contexts, managed facilities, and supplier-operated sites where emergency response coordination intersects with continuity, crisis management, security, privacy, or operational resilience.

---

## Protective action principles

1. Life safety takes priority over property, systems, records, and service restoration.
2. Local protective action may be required before executive or crisis management activation.
3. Emergency response must not expose individuals to unnecessary harm for evidence preservation or asset protection.
4. Communications must be factual, authorized, and appropriate to the emergency stage.
5. Response activity must transition to crisis management or continuity governance when disruption exceeds local response capability.
6. Public CC0 templates must not include facility addresses, floor plans, evacuation routes, emergency contacts, access codes, security procedures, or site-specific vulnerabilities.

---

## Emergency types and generic protective actions

| Emergency Type | Generic Protective Action |
| --- | --- |
| Fire or explosion | Evacuate where safe, activate local alarms, avoid re-entry until authorized, and account for personnel. |
| Medical emergency | Contact emergency services, provide assistance only within training and authority, protect privacy, and escalate through internal response channels. |
| Severe weather or natural hazard | Follow local safety direction, shelter or evacuate as appropriate, account for personnel, and report service impacts. |
| Security threat | Follow approved local threat response procedures, avoid confrontation, preserve life safety, and escalate to security or emergency services. |
| Hazardous material or environmental event | Avoid exposure, isolate area where safe, notify responsible response roles, and follow specialist direction. |
| Utility, facility, or infrastructure outage | Follow local safety procedures, assess operational impact, escalate continuity impact, and avoid unsafe workarounds. |
| Cyber or technology incident affecting physical operations | Prioritize safety, avoid unauthorized system changes, preserve evidence where safe, and coordinate with incident response. |
| AI or automation malfunction affecting physical or operational processes | Disable or bypass unsafe automation where authorized, preserve evidence where safe, and escalate to crisis and AI governance roles. |

---

## Role guidance

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Local Response Lead | Coordinates immediate local protective action and personnel accountability. |
| Facility or Site Owner | Maintains local emergency procedures, drills, and site-specific response records. |
| Security Owner | Coordinates physical security response and evidence preservation where applicable. |
| Health and Safety Owner | Coordinates safety requirements, first-aid readiness, and local emergency preparedness. |
| Crisis Manager | Assumes coordination when the event exceeds local response capability. |
| Communications Owner | Coordinates approved internal and external messaging. |
| Resilience Owner | Ensures that continuity impacts, corrective actions, and lessons learned are captured. |

---

## Evidence and review

Adopting organizations should maintain local emergency procedures, drill records, site risk assessments, training records, incident records, communication logs, corrective action records, and transition records into crisis or continuity processes.

---

## Limitations

This guideline is a public-domain baseline. It is not a substitute for site-specific emergency procedures, legal requirements, occupational health and safety obligations, law enforcement direction, emergency services direction, or facility-specific plans.
