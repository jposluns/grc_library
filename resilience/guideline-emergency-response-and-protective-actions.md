## Purpose

This guideline provides direction for protecting personnel, assets, and facilities during emergencies or disruptive events.  
It defines protective actions, communication procedures, and response coordination to ensure life safety and business continuity until the Crisis Management Team (CMT) assumes control.

---

## Scope

- Applies to all facilities, offices, warehouses, and operational sites.  
- Covers immediate physical protective actions required during natural disasters, fire, security incidents, or hazardous events.  
- Supports all employees, contractors, and visitors present at any organizational site.  
- Extends to regional offices, logistics hubs, and remote staff when operating under company supervision.

---

## Objectives

1. Protect life and ensure immediate safety during emergencies.  
2. Provide clear procedures for rapid decision-making and response.  
3. Establish consistent communication and accountability.  
4. Support transition from emergency response to continuity or crisis management operations.

---

## Emergency Response Principles

- **Life safety is the first priority**—protection of people precedes property and systems.  
- **Local response is immediate**—site personnel must act without waiting for executive authorization.  
- **Incident command structure (ICS)** ensures organized control, communication, and escalation.  
- **Continuous communication** keeps leadership informed while avoiding confusion or rumor.  
- **Preparedness through training** ensures confidence and coordinated actions under stress.

---

## Types of Emergencies and Immediate Actions

| Type | Examples | Immediate Protective Actions |
|------|-----------|------------------------------|
| **Fire / Explosion** | Office, warehouse, or equipment fire | Activate alarm, evacuate via nearest exit, close doors, assemble at designated point, notify authorities and security. |
| **Medical Emergency** | Serious injury, cardiac event, or illness | Call emergency services, provide first aid if trained, inform supervisor and security, maintain privacy. |
| **Natural Disaster** | Earthquake, flood, storm, or severe weather | Follow building safety signage, shelter in place or evacuate as directed, avoid windows, secure equipment. |
| **Security Threat** | Intruder, assault, bomb threat, or active shooter | Lockdown or evacuate depending on threat type, contact security and law enforcement, avoid confrontation. |
| **Chemical or Environmental Hazard** | Hazardous material spill, gas leak, or contamination | Evacuate area, avoid contact, alert security, and follow spill containment protocol. |
| **Power or System Outage** | Utility failure or prolonged outage | Remain calm, follow backup power protocols, await instructions from facility management or IT. |

## Evacuation and Shelter Procedures

1. **Evacuation**  
   - Follow posted evacuation routes and assembly points.  
   - Do not use elevators during fire or power outages.  
   - Supervisors perform roll call at assembly areas.  
   - Await “All Clear” notice from Security or Emergency Services.  

2. **Shelter-in-Place**  
   - Used for external threats (e.g., hazardous air, civil unrest).  
   - Close doors and windows, seal gaps if necessary.  
   - Move away from exterior walls and windows.  
   - Stay tuned to internal alerts until official stand-down.  

3. **Lockdown**  
   - Used during active threats or violence.  
   - Secure doors, silence devices, remain quiet and out of sight.  
   - Await instruction from law enforcement or Security Officer.

## Communication and Notification

- Emergency communications follow the chain of command outlined in the **Crisis Management & EOC Activation Procedure**.  
- Local sites use the emergency alert system, intercom, SMS, or Teams notification channels.  
- Security notifies the CISO, CIO, and CRO within one hour for any event requiring CMT consideration.  
- External communications (media, public, or regulators) are managed solely by the Communications Director or CEO as defined in the **Crisis Communication Plan**.

---

## Emergency Equipment and Supplies

- Each site must maintain:  
  - First-aid kits and automated external defibrillators (AEDs).  
  - Fire extinguishers and clearly marked exits.  
  - Emergency lighting and backup power for critical systems.  
  - Emergency contact lists and printed evacuation maps.  
  - Personal protective equipment (PPE) relevant to site operations.  
- Facility Managers inspect and replenish supplies quarterly.

---

## Training and Drills

- **Frequency**: Conduct evacuation drills at least twice per year; lockdown and shelter-in-place drills annually.  
- **Participants**: All employees and on-site contractors.  
- **Evaluation**: Document drill performance, timing, participation rate, and lessons learned.  
- **Improvement**: Findings are entered into the improvement tracking process for resolution and re-testing.

---

## Post-Emergency Actions

1. Conduct a **Post-Incident Review** within 5 business days.  
2. Record details including cause, response time, and corrective measures.  
3. Submit report to the Chief Security Officer and Enterprise Risk Committee.  
4. Provide counselling or support services for affected staff if required.  
5. Resume operations once authorized by the Crisis Management Team and facility management.
