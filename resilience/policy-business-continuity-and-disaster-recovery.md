## Purpose

This policy establishes a reusable governance baseline for business continuity, disaster recovery, crisis coordination, and recovery assurance. It defines policy intent for maintaining critical services, recovering data and systems, coordinating disruptive events, and preserving evidence for risk, compliance, audit, and improvement activities.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to business processes, information systems, cloud services, data stores, suppliers, facilities, workforce dependencies, AI systems, communication channels, and recovery capabilities that support critical operations.

---

## Policy Statements

1. Critical services and supporting dependencies must be identified through business impact analysis.
2. Recovery time objectives, recovery point objectives, maximum tolerable disruption, and minimum service levels must be defined for critical services.
3. Continuity and recovery plans must be maintained for processes, systems, data, suppliers, and facilities proportionate to impact.
4. Disaster recovery capability must be tested at a cadence proportionate to criticality and risk.
5. Recovery tests must record actual outcomes, failed assumptions, corrective actions, residual risk, and business acceptance.
6. Crisis management and communication processes must define activation criteria, role accountability, decision authority, communication approval, and evidence capture.
7. Supplier and cloud service dependencies must be assessed for continuity commitments, recovery capability, incident notification, concentration risk, and exit feasibility.
8. AI system continuity must address model service availability, retrieval store availability, data restoration, emergency disablement, fallback processes, monitoring records, and supplier-operated AI dependencies.
9. Backup and recovery controls must protect data confidentiality, integrity, availability, retention, and deletion obligations.
10. Exceptions to continuity or recovery requirements must be risk-assessed, approved, time-bound, and tracked to closure.

---

## Evidence Requirements

Adopting organizations should maintain business impact analyses, recovery objective registers, dependency maps, continuity plans, recovery plans, test reports, restoration logs, crisis action logs, supplier resilience evidence, corrective action logs, and risk acceptance records.

---

## Limitations

This policy does not determine legal, regulatory, contractual, or sector-specific continuity obligations. Applicability depends on jurisdiction, sector, customer commitments, criticality, supplier model, data categories, and operational facts.
