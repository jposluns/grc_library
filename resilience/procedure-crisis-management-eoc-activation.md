## Purpose

This procedure defines a reusable process for activating, operating, and deactivating a crisis management structure and emergency operations coordination capability during significant disruptive events.

---

## Scope

This procedure applies to major incidents and crises affecting operations, technology services, data, facilities, suppliers, workforce availability, customer commitments, legal obligations, privacy, security, AI systems, or public communications.

---

## Activation triggers

Activation should be considered when one or more conditions exist:

- Critical service outage or severe degradation.
- Material cybersecurity, privacy, safety, supplier, facility, or cloud service event.
- Data corruption, loss, leakage, or inability to restore required records.
- AI system failure, unsafe behaviour, unauthorized data exposure, or emergency disablement requirement.
- Disruption affecting customers, employees, suppliers, regulated activities, or critical operations.
- Event requiring executive coordination, legal review, regulatory assessment, or formal communication.
- Incident response or continuity activity requiring cross-functional prioritization.

---

## Procedure

### Step 1: Confirm event severity

Assess known facts, affected services, immediate risks, data impact, safety concerns, supplier involvement, legal or regulatory exposure, and required decision authority.

### Step 2: Activate crisis structure

Assign crisis roles, establish meeting cadence, open action and decision logs, define objectives, and initiate approved communication channels.

### Step 3: Stabilize operations

Prioritize life safety, containment, service continuity, evidence preservation, data protection, supplier escalation, and recovery sequencing.

### Step 4: Coordinate decisions

Track decisions, risk acceptances, communication approvals, restoration priorities, legal or compliance considerations, and resource constraints.

### Step 5: Monitor and report

Maintain current status, material changes, open actions, service impacts, recovery estimates, supplier status, and residual risk.

### Step 6: Deactivate

Deactivate the crisis structure when objectives are met, services are stabilized, communication requirements are complete, and residual activities are assigned to normal governance processes.

### Step 7: Review

Perform post-incident or post-exercise review. Record lessons, failed assumptions, corrective actions, evidence gaps, supplier issues, and required document updates.

---

## Minimum records

- Activation decision.
- Role assignments.
- Action log.
- Decision log.
- Situation reports.
- Communication approvals.
- Supplier escalation records.
- Recovery validation records.
- Residual risk decisions.
- Deactivation decision.
- Corrective action log.

---

## Limitations

This procedure is a public-domain baseline. Adopting organizations must define actual contact lists, meeting locations, communication channels, authority thresholds, legal escalation paths, and operational runbooks internally.
