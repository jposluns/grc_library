## Purpose

This register defines resilience terminology used across the public GRC Documentation Library. Definitions are original, organisation-neutral, and intended for CC0 reuse.

This register does not reproduce definitions from external standards, laws, trade programmes, or certification schemes.

---

## Terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Alternate Processing | A temporary method for continuing a process when the primary service, facility, system, supplier, or data source is unavailable. |
| Alternate Site | A pre-arranged physical or virtual location used to continue or restore critical activities when the primary location is unavailable. |
| Business Continuity | The capability to continue critical activities during disruption at an acceptable predefined level. |
| Business Continuity Plan | A documented plan describing how critical activities will continue during disruption. |
| Business Impact Analysis | A structured assessment identifying critical activities, dependencies, disruption impacts, recovery priorities, and recovery objectives. |
| Corrective Action | An action taken to address a failure, gap, nonconformity, or missed objective identified through testing, incident review, audit, or assessment. |
| Crisis | A significant event requiring coordinated decision-making beyond normal operational management. |
| Crisis Communication | The controlled process for preparing, approving, issuing, and recording communications during a crisis or material incident. |
| Crisis Management | Coordinated governance and operational activity used to manage a crisis, allocate resources, approve decisions, and direct response. |
| Critical Service | A service, process, system, supplier, facility, or data capability whose disruption could create material operational, legal, financial, safety, privacy, security, customer, or regulatory impact. |
| Degraded Mode | A temporary operating condition in which a service continues with reduced capability, performance, automation, integration, data freshness, or staffing. |
| Dependency Map | A record of people, systems, suppliers, facilities, data stores, communications, cloud services, AI systems, and integrations required to operate or recover a service. |
| Disaster Recovery | The restoration of technology services, data, configurations, infrastructure, applications, and related dependencies after disruption. |
| Emergency Operations Coordination | A structured coordination capability for response roles, status reporting, decisions, actions, communications, and evidence during a significant event. |
| Exercise | A planned activity used to evaluate readiness, roles, assumptions, communications, recovery capability, or response procedures. |
| Failover | Transfer of service operation from a primary capability to an alternate capability. |
| Fallback Procedure | A defined method for continuing operation when a primary system, service, supplier, AI system, or automation fails or becomes unsafe. |
| Impact Tolerance | The maximum level of disruption, degradation, loss, or delay that can be tolerated before impact becomes unacceptable. |
| Incident | An event that disrupts or may disrupt operations, security, privacy, resilience, safety, supplier performance, data integrity, or service availability. |
| Manual Workaround | A non-automated or alternate process used to continue critical activity during disruption. |
| Maximum Tolerable Disruption | The maximum duration or severity of disruption that can be tolerated before unacceptable impact occurs. |
| Minimum Service Level | The lowest level of service capability that must be sustained during disruption. |
| Post-Incident Review | A structured review after an incident or crisis to identify causes, decisions, failures, evidence gaps, corrective actions, and improvement opportunities. |
| Recovery Point Objective | The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured as time or transaction point. |
| Recovery Time Objective | The target time for restoring a process, system, service, data set, supplier capability, or operating function to an acceptable level. |
| Resilience | The capability to anticipate, prepare for, respond to, adapt to, and recover from disruptive events. |
| Restoration Validation | Confirmation that restored services, data, access, controls, integrations, monitoring, and business functions operate acceptably. |
| Return to Normal | The controlled transition from continuity, degraded, or crisis operations back to normal operating mode. |
| Supplier Resilience | The ability of an external provider or dependency to sustain, restore, or support critical services during disruption. |
| Tabletop Exercise | A discussion-based exercise used to review roles, decisions, assumptions, escalation, communication, and recovery logic. |
| Technical Restoration Test | A test that restores systems, data, configuration, or service capability to validate recoverability. |
| Trade and Supply-Chain Continuity | The ability to continue or recover trade, logistics, customs, shipment, routing, supplier, or cargo-related activities during disruption. |

## AI resilience terms

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| AI Emergency Disablement | A controlled method for stopping or restricting an AI system, model endpoint, tool, agent, retrieval source, or automation when behaviour becomes unsafe or unauthorized. |
| AI Fallback Process | A non-AI, alternate AI, manual, or degraded process used when an AI system is unavailable, unsafe, or outside approved operating conditions. |
| AI Service Dependency | A dependency on a model provider, inference service, retrieval store, orchestration layer, data pipeline, tool interface, or monitoring capability. |
| Retrieval Store Recovery | Restoration and validation of vector stores, indexed documents, search stores, memory stores, or other retrieval sources used by an AI system. |
| Tool Execution Boundary | The defined limit on actions that an AI system, agent, plugin, or automation can perform in external systems. |

## Maintenance rules

1. Definitions must be original and CC0-compatible.
2. Definitions must remain organisation-neutral.
3. Do not copy definitions from external standards, laws, or programme materials unless confirmed CC0-compatible.
4. New resilience terms should be added when they materially improve consistency across documents.
5. Role definitions should remain in [`governance/register-role-authority.md`](../governance/register-role-authority.md) unless a resilience-specific role definition is required.
