## Purpose

This register provides standardized definitions and acronyms for all Resilience-related artefacts, including business continuity, disaster recovery, crisis management, and trade-supply continuity.  
It ensures consistent terminology and traceability across all governance, operational, and compliance documentation.

---

## Definitions and Acronyms

| Term / Acronym | Definition |
|----------------|-------------|
| **AEO (Authorized Economic Operator)** | A global customs-program standard under WCO SAFE recognizing trusted, secure, and compliant traders. |
| **AEO-S** | Authorized Economic Operator – Security & Safety certification variant in the EU and UK. |
| **AI Governance Council (AIGC)** | Oversight body ensuring ethical, secure, and compliant use of artificial intelligence across business functions. |
| **Alternate Site** | A pre-arranged location used to restore critical operations if primary facilities are unavailable. |
| **ANNEX** | A supplementary governance document providing specialized control mappings or requirements. |
| **ATT (Australian Trusted Trader)** | Australia’s customs-security and supply-chain program aligned with WCO SAFE mutual recognition. |
| **Audit Committee** | Governance body that reviews assurance, compliance, and risk management performance. |
| **BASC (Business Alliance for Secure Commerce)** | International trade-security program for logistics, customs, and supply-chain continuity. |
| **BCM (Business Continuity Manager)** | The individual responsible for coordinating business-continuity planning, testing, and program governance. |
| **BCMS (Business Continuity Management System)** | The management system that implements, maintains, and continually improves organizational resilience (ISO 22301). |
| **BCP (Business Continuity Plan)** | A documented procedure outlining how an organization will continue critical operations during disruption. |
| **BCR (Business Continuity & Resilience)** | The collective governance and operational framework that ensures continuity, recovery, and sustainability. |
| **BIA (Business Impact Analysis)** | The process for identifying critical business functions, dependencies, and the impact of disruption. |
| **CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)** | Actions taken to eliminate causes of nonconformities and prevent recurrence. |
| **CCT (Crisis Communication Team)** | The designated team responsible for managing crisis messaging, stakeholder updates, and media coordination. |
| **CEO / CIO / CRO / CISO / CCO / CLO / GC / CSO** | Executive roles defined in the Role Authority Register; refer there for detailed role descriptions. |
| **Cloud Service Provider (CSP)** | Third-party vendor providing cloud-hosted infrastructure, platform, or software services. |
| **COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies)** | ISACA governance framework for enterprise IT and risk management. |
| **Compliance Management System (CMS)** | The integrated processes for identifying, monitoring, and fulfilling regulatory obligations (ISO 37301). |
| **Continuity Exercise** | A planned simulation to validate the effectiveness of business continuity and disaster recovery plans. |
| **Crisis** | A significant, often unplanned event requiring executive decision-making and coordination beyond normal operations. |
| **Crisis Management Team (CMT)** | Executive-level group activated to oversee enterprise crisis response, resource allocation, and recovery. |
| **Critical Infrastructure** | Essential systems and assets whose disruption would have significant operational or societal impact. |
| **CTPAT (Customs Trade Partnership Against Terrorism)** | U.S. Customs and Border Protection’s supply-chain security program for trusted traders. |
| **Cybersecurity Incident** | Any unauthorized event compromising information systems, networks, or data integrity. |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | The designated individual responsible for data privacy compliance and breach reporting. |
| **Disaster Recovery (DR)** | The restoration of IT systems, infrastructure, and data after a disruption. |
| **Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)** | The documented strategy and step-by-step procedures for restoring IT and data services. |
| **EAC AEO Program** | East African Community’s Authorized Economic Operator initiative aligning with WCO SAFE. |
| **EOC (Emergency Operations Center)** | The physical or virtual command center for crisis coordination and decision-making. |
| **ERC (Enterprise Risk Committee)** | Governance body overseeing enterprise risk, continuity, and assurance performance. |
| **Exercise** | A planned, structured activity (tabletop, simulation, or full-scale) to evaluate recovery readiness. |
| **Framework** | A governance-level artefact defining structure, responsibilities, and integration of standards. |
| **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** | European Union regulation governing data privacy and breach notification. |
| **Guideline** | Advisory or instructional document outlining best practices or templates. |
| **Incident** | An unplanned event that disrupts normal business or IT operations. |
| **Incident Response (IR)** | The coordinated set of actions for identifying, analyzing, containing, eradicating, and recovering from incidents. |
| **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** | Framework for managing and protecting information assets (ISO 27001). |
| **ISO 22301** | Standard for Business Continuity Management Systems. |
| **ISO 22320** | Standard for Emergency Management and Incident Response. |
| **ISO 22361** | Standard for Crisis Management – Guidelines for Strategic Capability. |
| **ISO 22398** | Standard for Exercise Programs and Testing. |
| **ISO 27001 / 27031 / 27035 / 27701 / 28000** | Core ISO standards covering information security, ICT readiness, incident management, privacy, and supply-chain security. |
| **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** | Quantitative metric used to measure performance or compliance with resilience objectives. |
| **Lessons Learned** | Documented insights from incidents or exercises used to improve future responses. |
| **MEAC (Monitor, Evaluate, Assess, and Correct)** | COBIT performance and improvement principle applied to resilience testing. |
| **NEEC (Nuevo Esquema de Empresas Certificadas)** | Mexico’s customs certification program equivalent to AEO. |
| **OEA (Operador Económico Autorizado)** | Brazil’s Authorized Economic Operator program aligned with WCO SAFE and BASC. |
| **OECD (Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development)** | International organization establishing policy frameworks, including 2026 Digital Security Principles. |
| **PIP (Partners in Protection)** | Canadian customs-security and trade-continuity program under CBSA, aligned with WCO SAFE and CTPAT. |
| **Plan Activation** | The formal process of enacting a continuity or crisis management plan following an incident. |
| **Post-Incident Review (PIR)** | A structured evaluation following an incident to determine root cause and identify improvements. |
| **RPO (Recovery Point Objective)** | The maximum tolerable data loss measured in time. |
| **RTO (Recovery Time Objective)** | The targeted timeframe for restoring a process or system. |
| **Register** | A recordkeeping artefact for tracking metrics, actions, contacts, or events. |
| **Resilience** | The organizational capability to anticipate, absorb, respond to, and recover from disruptive events. |
| **Risk Appetite** | The degree of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives. |
| **Role Authority Register** | Meta-governance document defining organizational roles, authorities, and accountability boundaries. |
| **Simulation Test** | A hands-on, operational validation of recovery processes in real or near-real conditions. |
| **SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)** | A procedural document detailing step-by-step operational actions. |
| **Tabletop Exercise** | Discussion-based review of response procedures using scenario-based questioning. |
| **Trade and Supply-Chain Continuity** | The ability of logistics, customs, and supply partners to sustain operations during disruption. |
| **WCO SAFE (World Customs Organization SAFE Framework)** | Global standard for customs cooperation, trade-security, and AEO mutual recognition. |
