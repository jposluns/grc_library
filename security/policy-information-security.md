## Purpose

This policy establishes a reusable information security governance baseline for protecting confidentiality, integrity, availability, accountability, and resilience of systems, services, data, identities, networks, suppliers, and AI-enabled capabilities.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to information systems, cloud services, endpoints, networks, identities, applications, data stores, logs, suppliers, AI systems, development environments, production services, backups, and administrative tooling.

---

## Policy Statements

1. Information security requirements must be risk-based, documented, approved, implemented, monitored, and evidenced.
2. Access to systems and data must be authorized, role-appropriate, logged where required, periodically reviewed, and revoked when no longer required.
3. Sensitive data must be classified, protected, retained, transferred, and deleted according to defined requirements.
4. Systems must produce security-relevant logs sufficient to support investigation, monitoring, incident response, audit, and accountability.
5. Vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, and exposed services must be identified, prioritized, remediated, or accepted through formal risk governance.
6. Security controls must be applied across architecture, design, acquisition, development, deployment, operation, monitoring, and retirement.
7. Incident response must include identification, triage, containment, eradication, recovery, communication, evidence handling, and lessons learned.
8. Suppliers and cloud services must be assessed for security, privacy, resilience, data handling, incident notification, and exit obligations.
9. AI systems must be governed for data leakage, prompt injection, unsafe tool use, data poisoning, model inversion, membership inference, shadow AI, provenance, lineage, retention, and deletion risk.
10. Exceptions to security requirements must be documented, time-bound, risk accepted, and monitored.

---

## Evidence Expectations

Evidence may include policies, standards, risk assessments, control tests, access reviews, logging records, vulnerability records, incident records, supplier reviews, architecture decisions, AI impact assessments, exceptions, and remediation records.

---

## Limitations

This policy is a public-domain baseline. Adopting organizations must validate legal, regulatory, contractual, sector, and architectural requirements before operational use.
