## Purpose

This procedure defines the process for investigating and responding to security policy violations, including graduated disciplinary actions proportionate to severity. It ensures that security breaches, misuse of assets, and non-compliance events are handled consistently, fairly, and in accordance with employment law and regulatory obligations.

---

## Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors, temporary workers, and third parties who access organizational systems or data and who are subject to the organization's security policies. Covers violations of any security policy, standard, or procedure within the GRC library.

---

## Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **CISO** | Initiates security investigations; determines severity classification; advises HR on security context. |
| **HR / People Operations** | Owns the disciplinary process; ensures that procedural fairness and legal compliance. |
| **Legal Counsel** | Advises on employment law, regulatory obligations, and any criminal referral. |
| **Manager / People Leader** | Participates in disciplinary meetings; implements agreed outcomes. |
| **Internal Audit** | Reviews disciplinary process adherence and documents systemic issues. |

---

## 1. Violation categories

Security violations are classified by severity:

| Severity | Examples | Typical Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Low** | Unintentional policy breach with no data exposure; failure to complete required training; minor acceptable-use violation | Informal counselling; mandatory training |
| **Medium** | Intentional policy bypass; sharing credentials; accessing data outside role; failure to report a suspected incident | Formal written warning; additional training; access restriction |
| **High** | Deliberate data exfiltration; installing unauthorized software that creates material risk; repeated medium violations; failure to report a known breach | Final written warning; suspension; access revocation |
| **Critical** | Insider threat; sabotage; fraud; criminal conduct; wilful exposure of sensitive data | Summary dismissal; referral to law enforcement; regulatory notification |

---

## 2. Incident detection and referral

2.1 Security violations may be detected via: SIEM alerts, DLP triggers, access control anomalies, manager or colleague reports, self-disclosure, or audit findings.

2.2 The detecting party refers the matter to the CISO and HR immediately.

2.3 The CISO performs an initial assessment within 2 business days to determine:
- Whether a violation occurred.
- Preliminary severity classification.
- Whether immediate access suspension is required to protect systems or evidence.

2.4 Immediate access suspension does not constitute a disciplinary action and must be communicated as a precautionary measure.

---

## 3. Investigation

3.1 The CISO leads the security investigation. HR participates where the investigation may lead to disciplinary action.

3.2 Investigation activities may include:
- Log and audit trail review.
- Device or endpoint forensic review.
- Interviews with relevant parties.
- Review of access records.
- Review of data movement and DLP events.

3.3 Investigation findings are documented in a confidential investigation report.

3.4 The subject of investigation is informed of the investigation before it commences, unless doing so would compromise evidence integrity. In that case, they are informed as soon as practicable.

3.5 Investigation timelines:
- Initial findings: within 5 business days.
- Full investigation report: within 15 business days (may be extended for complex cases).

---

## 4. Disciplinary hearing

4.1 If the investigation identifies a potential violation, HR initiates the disciplinary process in accordance with employment law.

4.2 The subject receives written notice of the allegation, supporting evidence, and the right to be accompanied by a colleague or representative.

4.3 A disciplinary hearing is held where the subject may respond to findings.

4.4 HR makes the disciplinary determination with security input from the CISO.

---

## 5. Disciplinary outcomes

5.1 Outcomes are proportionate to severity and circumstances:

| Outcome | Trigger |
| --- | --- |
| No action / coaching | Violation not established or was minor and unintentional |
| Informal warning | Low-severity first offence |
| Formal written warning | Medium-severity or repeated low-severity violations |
| Final written warning | High-severity first offence or repeated medium violations |
| Dismissal | Critical violation or repeated high-severity violations |
| Referral to law enforcement | Criminal conduct |
| Regulatory notification | Data breach or regulatory violation requiring notification |

5.2 All disciplinary outcomes are documented and retained by HR per the Data Retention Schedule.

---

## 6. Appeal

6.1 The subject has the right to appeal any formal disciplinary outcome within 5 business days.

6.2 Appeals are heard by a senior leader not involved in the original investigation.

6.3 The appeal decision is final and documented.

---

## 7. Post-action security controls

7.1 Following any formal disciplinary action, the CISO reviews whether additional access controls, monitoring, or remediation steps are required.

7.2 Systemic issues identified through disciplinary proceedings are documented in the CAPA Register and considered in the next security awareness programme review.
